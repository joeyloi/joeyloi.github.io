Reading
I don't read as much as I ought to, but I'm working on that. This running list highlights the books I've enjoyed and found insightful.



The Signal and the Noise

Nate Silver

I picked up this book because it brings together three topics that are relevant and intersting to me: data science, statistics, and psychology. Still in the middle of this one, I'm looking to gain insights on different ways to use data to make predictions about the future.


Zero to One

Peter Thiel with Blake Masters

Thiel offers a glimpse of what it's like to reason about the world from a first princples perspective. In matters of startups, technology, and building the future, it is clear to me now that this framework offers the most logical and productive way of proceeding. This book has informed how I frame problems and develop ideas and actions to solve them.


Letters from a Stoic

Seneca

I still regularly flip through and read certain passages to help me frame and subdue my anxieties. Seneca discusses a considered and deliberate way of life that demands emotional discipline. The reward of such a living is a resilience that allows one to find resolve through any of life's hardships.


Outliers

Malcolm Gladwell

Apart from its celebrated insights (re: 10,000 hours, looking beyond inherent talents and instead to circumstance as contributors to success, etc.), I found the breadth of stories and trivia to be really entertaining. This one's great to listen to as an audiobook (as I did); Galdwell is lively and captivating in the way he tells his stories.


The Art of War

Sun Tzu

Most fascinating to me were the discussions regarding morale and the relationships between rank. Beyond the context of military strategy, the ideas here are incredibly relevent to leadership and business management.
